WElLNESS

College is stressful so it is very important for me to have ways to manage the stress and take care of my mental health! Below are just a few example of what I do at college to lower my anxiety and stress.

Meditation

My main way of taking care of my mental health is through meditation. I start and end every day with a guided meditation on the Calm app shown above. I find that meditation is a great way for me to clear my head and release any tension from the day.

In addition to meditation, I also practice Emotional Freedom Techniques (EFT) throughout the day when I am feeling anxious or stressed. Tapping allows me to move past these stressful or anxious situations and continue on with my day.

While meditating I like to burn sage to cleanse my room and space of all negative energy. In addition to sage, I sometimes burn seagrass and palo santo wood.

Hiking

Hiking has always been an important part of my life ever since I was little. My mom would take us on mandatory family hikes every weekend when I was young. I have grown to love hiking because it allows me to be in nature and get away from the stress of school for a couple hours! It has been super fun finding new hiking spots in Michigan.

Friends

Handing out with my friends is super important for my mental health. College can be very stressful so having friends to support you is very important. I love to cook dinner or go out to a movie with my friends on the weekend.

Sports

Above is a photo of me playing tennis. I love to play tennis to release stress and get exercise. I go play tennis with my friends next to Palmer field. In addition to tennis, I love to surf in LA. I go super early in the morning with my Dad or friends. Unfortunately there is no surf in Ann Arbor, but I joined the lake surfing club at Michigan.
